Food complaint

  Before you continue

Not all food businesses in Queensland need a licence. A food business does not need a licence for the sale of:

  • packaged food
  • whole fruit and vegetables
  • drinks (except fruit and vegetable juice processed on site)
  • ice, including flavoured ice
  • seeds, spices, dried herbs, tea leaves, coffee or ground coffee
  • unpackaged snack food (for example, confectionary, potato chips, corn chips or nuts)
  • food sold by non-profit organisations for fundraising (for example, sausage sizzle or cake stall)
  • food provided at state government run facilities (for example, a public hospital or state school)

Licensed food businesses are required by law to display their licence or a copy of their licence so that customers can easily see it. The expiry date is shown on the licence so you can see if the licence is current.

  Intentional contamination

If you suspect a food product has been intentionally contaminated call 13 HEALTH (13 43 25 84).

Intentional contamination of food means contamination by a person who intends to contaminate the food, including by adding, mixing or putting a harmful or poisonous substance or thing into the food.

Use this complaint type when you asked the food handler to prepare or handle your food so that the food was safe for your food allergy.

You should always tell a food business about your food allergy when ordering food. The food business should tell you:

  • If the food is safe for you to eat
  • If the food contains ingredients or has been handled in a way that is unsafe for your food allergy
  • When they are not able to make or handle the food safely for your food allergy.

  Tongs, gloves and hairnets

Food handlers do not have to use tongs or wear gloves or hairnets when handling food.

All food handlers must prevent the contamination of food, but how they do this is up to them. For example, if they wash their hands regularly, in between tasks and each time they handle food, they don’t need to wear gloves.

For further information, read about the responsibilities of food handlers.

  We do not investigate this complaint

Poor customer service cannot be investigated under the Food Act 2006.

Contact the food business directly to report poor customer service. For example, missing food items or delivery through a third party delivery service. For more information, read about how to make a consumer complaint to a food business.

  Do you have food allergies?

If you have a food allergy: Use this complaint type when you asked for a food ingredient to be removed because of a food allergy.

If you do not have a food allergy: Discuss this complaint with the food business, as this may be an issue with their customer service or ordering system and does not impact the safety of the food. For more information, read about how to make a consumer complaint to a food business.

  Before you continue

Use by dates and best before dates must be displayed on all packaged food with a shelf life of 2 years or less.

It is not safe to eat food after the use by date. Food must not be sold after its use by date.

Foods with a best before date can be sold and eaten past the date.
