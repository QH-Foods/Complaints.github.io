Complaint Details

Food poisoning illness details




Diarrhoea is defined as three or more loose, liquid or unformed stools (poo) within a 24 hour period.
Bloody diarrhoea is defined as three or more loose, liquid or unformed stools (poo) within a 24 hour period where red blood is seen at least once. You may also a see sticky fluid called mucus.
This does not include when you attempt to throw up but can’t.
Pain or ache in the head.
If you have a fever, you may have any of the following: feeling significantly hot/ flushed (not related to the weather), body sweats at rest, chills (or shivering).

Medical advice

For example. did you see a doctor, or go to the hospital?
Your doctor may have asked you to provide a sample so they can work out what type of food poisoning you have. This sample is usually either a vomit or stool (poo) sample, depending on your symptoms. This does not include a blood test.

Food details








Food samples

info   Important information

It is important the food package or leftover food is stored safely in case an environmental health officer needs the items for further investigation.

  1. Keep the food in the original packaging (if available)
  2. Place the food in a container that you can tightly seal
  3. Follow the manufacturer’s instructions to see if the leftover food should be stored in the cupboard, fridge or freezer.
